Who am I?

I am Michela Parma, student of Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge at the University of Bologna.

Creativity, curiosity and interest in design have led me to this creative and communication medium that is the web. My course of study has changed a lot over the years. I have gone from love for architecture to love for History. I have never denied either of them because both have allowed me to understand what I really like to do: create websites and enrich them with content. On the one hand the sensitivity for design, color matching and the choice of images, and on the other hand the skills of historical research.
I like to call myself Brutalist lover. Brutalism is a design current that applies to both architecture and web development: geometric, rough and visually heavy elements that are reflected in my creative choices.

What I love about web design

I like to see how a project comes to life, from the original idea to the design on paper and, finally, to the technological implementation. I like to watch the project from beginning to end, and have people test the finished product. I like to study human behavior in relation to computers, the conscious and subconscious choices of people using a tool and the needs of users. Finally, I like the infinite possibilities offered by digital support.

Other interests

I like singing, creating tunes, acting, baking cakes and I love chihuahuas.

Team project developed for the course of Knowledge Representation and Extraction (University of Bologna). DIV-O stands for Dante Inferno V - Ontology. The project presents a tool to explore the Canto V through four differet editions of Divina commedia, according to characters and places, both real and fictional, and literary references present in the Canto. It's also possible to analise the relations identified in the Canto V through the Knowledge Graph.

Team project developed for the course of Web Writing and Digital Storytelling (University of Bologna). It concerns a serious game designed to make known the history of the Italian Monument Man, Rodolfo Siviero. The game consists of an escape room located inside Casa Siviero, in Florence. Players are called to act as secret collaborators of Siviero in order to save an important Italian work of art from the capture of Nazis. Through a series of puzzles and enigmas to solve, participants will have to contact the authorities to hide the painting and conclude the game.
